Kyle's Design Principles

Click the arrows to expand each category.

Key Principles ▶

  • Visibility - of functions, instructions, etc
  • Consistentcy
  • Feedback
  • Constraints
  • Affordance - "to give a clue" - an attribute of an object that allows people to know how to use it.
  • Accessibility

Usability Goals ▶

  • Effectiveness
  • Efficiency
  • Safety
  • Utility
  • Learnability
  • Memorability
  • Asthetically-pleasing design is often interpreted as being more user-friendly

Complexity Considerations ▶

  • Easy as email
  • Allow users to learn by doing
  • Use icons, but be clear about what they mean
  • Don't make the user think
  • Break complex tasks into smaller steps
  • Be careful not to simplify to the point of abstraction
  • Avoid overwhelming users by highlighting recommended options
  • Minimize choices, especially when response times are critical

Cultural Considerations ▶

  • Make sure not to include offensive symbols/icons
  • Design for your target market (gender, race, etc.)

Conceptual Models ▶

  • Design the conceptual model purposely--don't let it arise from the technology
  • Be wary of poor or erroneous model
  • Make sure the mental model and the conceptual model match
  • Metaphors need to have afoordances and constraints

Jakob's Law ▶

  • Users spend most of their time on other sites. This means that users prefer your site to work the same way as all the other sites they already know
  • When we want to make hanges, minimize discord by empowering users to continue using a previous version
  • Legacy problem - too many devices use the existing standard.

Human-Centered Design Principles ▶

  • Provide discoverability
  • Provide feedback
  • Utilize a conceptual model
  • Include affordances, signifiers, constraints
  • Ensure you have good mappings - the relationship between controls and their actions

Emotional/Accessibility Principles ▶

  • Clear instructions
  • Poll differently abled people
  • Add features that will help people depending on issue

Smartphone Principles ▶

  • Make items large enough to tap with a finger
  • Make your page fit within the viewport of a phone
  • Make it intuitive to use with a touch screen (minimal or no hovering)
  • Scale the page to be visible
  • Organize the screen for viewing and interacting with on a small screen

Hick's Law ▶

  • The time it takes to make a decision increases with the number and complexity of choices available
  • RT = a + b log2 (n)
    1. RT = reaction time
    2. n is the number of stimuli
    3. a and b are constants
